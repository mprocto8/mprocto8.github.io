Information Architecture · Proposal v2

Less choices.
More learning.

A proposed restructure of Thai Learner from a feature-rich dashboard to a focused four-tab architecture. Built around how returning users actually behave: they want to know what to do next, not what they could do.

Current 5 tabs · multiple CTAs · dashboard-style
Proposed 4 tabs · 1 primary CTA · session-style
Optimized for Retention · habit · monetization
The proposal

Four tabs, one purpose each.

Each tab answers a different question the user is asking themselves. Home answers "what should I do now?" Learn answers "where am I in my journey?" Library answers "let me explore." Settings stays out of the way.

Settings

Out of the way. Voice, display, notifications, account. Premium status visible. Notifications enable daily habit-building.

The home screen

Why one primary action.

Research on language app retention shows the same pattern repeatedly: clarity beats abundance. Users who see one clear action take it 3-5x more often than users who see five options and pick none.

The retention insight

Streaks don't drive learning. They drive 30-second busywork that "saves the streak." What drives learning is the visible feeling that you can now do something you couldn't yesterday. The "You can now..." section makes mastery tangible — concrete capabilities, not abstract XP.

i.

One primary CTA

"Today's Session" picks the right next thing based on user state. Mistake review if needed, otherwise next pathway step. No paralysis.

ii.

Visible capability growth

Concrete unlocks ("Ask for things politely" → next) replace abstract XP. The user sees themselves becoming functional in Thai.

iii.

Secondary options de-emphasized

Mistake review and free play remain accessible but smaller. They serve users who want them without dominating the primary path.

iv.

Light touchpoints

Streak count, word of the day, day number — present but not pressuring. Habit-forming without anxiety-inducing.

What gets demoted

The current home screen treats streak, XP, daily drill, mistake count, and word of the day as roughly equal priority. The new structure makes session continuation the top priority, with everything else as supporting context. This is the single biggest UX change.

Monetization-ready

Built to pay for itself.

The new architecture creates natural moments for premium upsell — without gating the core learning experience. Pay for momentum, not content.

i.

Premium voice slot

Settings → Voice picker shows Serafina as a premium option. Already built. Becomes a paid feature once Stripe is wired up.

ii.

Soft daily caps

Free users get ~15 min/day of "Today's Session." Premium removes the cap. Caps drive upgrades without limiting access.

iii.

Premium-only modes

Future Conversation Simulator and advanced analytics live in Library but are gated. Real value differentiation.

iv.

Account tier visible

Premium status appears in user pill (Settings). Light status signaling. Free users see what they could unlock.

Implementation

Ship in three phases.

Each phase ships independently and leaves the app working. No big-bang rewrites.

1.

Topic detail screen

Build the topic browser improvement first. Works in current IA. Becomes the foundation for the Library tab later.

2.

IA restructure

Rename tabs, reorganize content. Big visual change but mostly moving things between places.

3.

Smart Home logic

Build the "Today's Session" algorithm. Capability tracking. Personalized recommendations. The intelligence layer.
